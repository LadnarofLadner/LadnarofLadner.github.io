HOW IS MY RESEARCH, AFTER THE ELECTION OF 2024? It is going pretty well. I have eschewed, for about 20 years, the "sexy research", wherein, young graduate students follow directly in their mentors footsteps, embracing their pet projects, and including, almost invariably, the "buzz words" in their grant proposals, so as to rain down upon the chosen few, the choicest of government grants, foundation support, and corporate funding. Not that I am opposed, entirely, to that way of doing things, but it just isn't my style and never was. I prefer to think independently, and speak my mind, create freely and unencumbered, and, basically, to retain the freedom to chart my own course, in my own research. But, alas, I find I have succumbed to the utilization of a few buzz words, and even enjoy the papers these colleagues have published over the previous 20 years. I will, very specifically, be using three cutting-edge technologies that I have no right to claim to have furthered, myself, but hope to make such contributions, in the near future. Chief among these, will be : (A) DNA and olfactory receptor research, (B) Nanodisk research, (C) Microfluidics. And, as of 2025, and late-2024, I find such things as Cryo-EM, MINFLUX, and other fluorescent microscopy and labelling techniques to be intriguing avenues for the exploration of (1) protein structures and (2) for the de-orphanization and the study of the change of protein structures when in the presence of agonist and antagonist molecules.
