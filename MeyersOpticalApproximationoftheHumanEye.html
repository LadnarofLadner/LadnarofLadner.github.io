As Adler once declared "Euclid Dethroned, Euclid Triumphant", we might wish to reassess this pronouncement, given that he made it some ten or so years after the first translation of Euclid's "Optics" into the English language, and at a similar temporal distance, too, to Lunenberg's Non-Euclidean analyses of human vision. Indeed, an empirical view of the parameters of human vision might be more illuminating to our modern perspective than the more abstruse and mathematical, theoretical perspectives offered by the mathemagicians I've cited.
