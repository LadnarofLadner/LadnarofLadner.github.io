A quick synopsis, to be elaborated upon at some later date (today is 05/05/2026), will just provide a list of the three anatomical papers, known to me, wherein a comprehensive survey, or rather, a determined effort to obtain a good statistical approximation of the density and size and location of the four various photoreceptors (3 types of cone cells and one kind of rod cell) in the human animal, was attmepted. Several of these studies mention the difficulty of preseved specimens drying out, necessitating a sub-sampling procedure, counting variouus small regions and multiplying to characterize the larger area. The three studies I am aware of are Osterberg (1935), Curcio et. al. (1991), and Jonas et. al. (1994).

Off the top of my head, from memory, without looking at the sources, Osterberg estimated about 120 million rod cells, and about 6.5 million cone cells. Curcio et. al. put the estimate closer to 90 million rods cells, and 4.5 million cone cells. Jonas and their group sampled several whole retinas and put the figures closer to 60 million rods and 3 million cone cells. Jonas is the paper I am least familiar with, having found it just a year or two ago, and perused it only very casually. I hope to add some more concrete comments here, at some later, more opportune date and time.
